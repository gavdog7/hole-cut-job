Midea Duo Wall Vent Installation

Cupboard install • 6⅛" hole • Through-wall exhaust • Taupe board-and-batten exterior

Job Summary

What You're Doing

Cutting a 6⅛" diameter hole through your cupboard wall (drywall + wood studs) to permanently vent your Midea Duo MAP14HS1TBL portable air conditioner. The AC lives inside the cupboard; the exhaust exits through the wall to the outside.

Key Advantage — Hose-in-Hose Design

The Midea Duo uses a single hose-in-hose system: hot exhaust goes out through the inner channel while fresh intake air comes in through the outer channel. This means one hole, one hose — but it also means you cannot use a standard backdraft damper (it would block the intake). Plan for a screen-only vent or removable damper.

Important: Which Parts to Use (and Which to Skip)

The Midea Duo comes with multiple adapters. For a through-wall install, you do NOT use the rectangular Sliding Window Adapter (the ~12" × 8" two-piece plastic shell). That piece is designed only for fitting the hose into window slider tracks.

For your wall install, you use:

  • The round hose (~5" diameter, hose-in-hose)
  • The round Air Exhaust Adapter (circular ring that clips onto the hose end)
  • A 6⅜" round hole through the wall with a 6" duct sleeve

The hose is round. The hole is round. The rectangular adapter stays in the box.

Critical: Cupboard Ventilation

The #1 mistake people make with closet AC installs: the unit needs to pull room air in and blow cooled air out. If the cupboard doors are closed, it'll just recirculate closet air and overheat. Plan to keep doors open, install louvered doors, or cut vents into the existing doors.

Why 6⅛"?

A 6" rigid metal duct sleeve needs a 6⅛" hole — this is the industry-standard clearance that gives you just enough room to slide the sleeve through while keeping it snug enough to seal with foam and caulk. The Midea Duo's round hose assembly fits inside a 6" duct.

Don't Be Confused by the 12" × 8" Rectangular Adapter

The Midea Duo kit includes a Sliding Window Adapter (~12" × 8" rectangular two-piece plastic shell). This is NOT used for a through-wall install — it's designed only for fitting the round hose into a window slider panel track. The actual hose that goes through your wall is round, ~5" diameter. A 6⅜" hole is the correct cut.

Stud Bay Clearance

Standard stud spacing is 16" on center, giving you a clear bay of about 14.5" between studs. Your 6⅛" hole fits easily with plenty of margin. Use your stud finder to locate the studs and pick the center of a bay.

Height Positioning

The exhaust port on the Midea Duo is on the back of the unit, roughly 12–16" off the floor. Unbox the unit first and measure the exact center of the exhaust port, then mark your hole at that height so the hose runs straight and short.

  • Shorter hose = better efficiency
  • No kinks or bends allowed
  • Manual says: no objects within 20" of inlet/outlet hose
  • Slight downward slope to exterior (¼") helps with condensation
Stud bay clearance — your 6⅜" hole fits comfortably in the ~14.5" clear bay

Step-by-Step Walkthrough

0

Prep & Clear the Cupboard

  • Remove the HOTAS shelf and all gear — you need clear access to the back wall
  • Unbox the Midea Duo and measure the exact height of the exhaust port from the floor
  • Decide if the shelf goes back above the unit later or gets removed (unit is 32.5" tall)
  • Lay down a drop cloth — drywall dust gets everywhere
1

Locate Your Cut Point

  • Stud finder: Map the studs behind the drywall. Use wire detection mode — the outlet means wires exist in this wall
  • Pick a stud bay: Center your hole in a bay (~14.5" clear). The 6⅜" hole fits with plenty of margin
  • Mark the height: Match the center of the Midea's exhaust port (roughly 12–16" from floor — measure your unit!)
  • Mark the circle: Trace around the hole saw pilot point, or use a compass at 3-3/16" radius
Watch Out

Scan for wires AND plumbing. The outlet in the cupboard means electrical runs through this wall. Stay well clear — at least 1.5" from any detected wires.

2

Cut from Inside (Drywall)

  • Mount the 6⅜" hole saw on your drill
  • Position the pilot bit on your center mark
  • Drill slowly and steadily through the drywall — the hole saw does the work, don't force it
  • The pilot bit will punch through the entire wall — this is your alignment point for the outside
  • Pull out the drywall disc
  • Clear any insulation from the cavity
  • If your hole saw can't reach through the full wall depth, that's fine — you'll finish from outside
3

Cut from Outside (Sheathing + Siding)

  • Go outside and find the pilot hole from Step 2
  • Center the hole saw pilot bit in the pilot hole
  • Drill through the sheathing (OSB/plywood) and siding with the same hole saw
  • For board-and-batten, go slowly on the visible surface
  • If the siding is too thick for the hole saw, use your oscillating multi-tool to finish the cut
  • Angle tip: Very slight downward slope (¼") toward exterior helps with condensation drainage
4

Install the Duct Sleeve

  • Measure your wall depth (drywall face to siding face)
  • Cut the 6" rigid metal duct to that length with tin snips or hacksaw
  • Insert the sleeve through the hole from inside
  • It should sit flush or very slightly proud on both sides
  • Interior seal: Apply expanding foam around the sleeve where it meets the drywall — fill the gap completely
  • Let the foam cure, then trim flush with a utility knife
Why the Metal Sleeve?

The Midea's exhaust air carries humidity. Without a metal sleeve, that moist air would contact exposed wood framing inside the wall, eventually causing rot. The sleeve protects the wood and creates a clean, sealed passage.

5

Mount the Exterior Vent Cap

  • Apply a bead of clear silicone caulk around the duct opening on the exterior wall
  • Press the vent cap flange into the caulk
  • Secure with stainless steel screws (included with most vents) into the sheathing/siding
  • Apply another bead of caulk around the outside edge of the flange
  • Smooth with a wet finger for a clean line
  • For InOvate 6WVK: Pin or remove the damper so the Duo's intake airflow isn't blocked
  • For FAMCO/Broan: Remove the backdraft damper entirely, keep the screen
6

Seal the Interior Connection

  • Attach the Midea Duo's included wall exhaust adapter to the interior face of the duct sleeve
  • Seal the connection with HVAC foil tape — wrap it tight, no gaps
  • Connect the hose-in-hose assembly from the AC unit to the wall adapter
  • Keep the hose as short and straight as possible — no kinks, no extensions
  • Verify no objects within 20" of the inlet and outlet per the manual
7

Test & Commission

  • Plug in the Midea Duo
  • Turn it on and run for 10 minutes
  • Go outside — verify you feel hot exhaust from the vent
  • Check inside for any air leaks around the wall adapter (feel with your hand)
  • If any leaks, add more foil tape
  • Check that cool air is flowing out the top of the unit into the room
8

Cupboard Ventilation (Don't Skip!)

  • The AC pulls room air in from the front grille and blows cooled air out the top
  • If cupboard doors are closed, it recirculates a tiny air volume and overheats
  • Option A: Remove the doors entirely
  • Option B: Install louvered doors
  • Option C: Cut vent openings (top and bottom) into existing doors
  • Top vent lets cool air out; bottom vent lets room air in
9

Winter Seal

  • When the AC season is over, disconnect the hose from the wall adapter
  • Cut a magnetic vent cover to size (slightly larger than the vent cap)
  • Stick it over the exterior vent — the magnet holds it against the steel vent cap
  • Pops on/off in seconds, costs ~$3, and is barely visible on dark siding
